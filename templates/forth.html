Tips to manage & prevent PCOS
Balanced diet
Reduce refined carbs and sugar. Focus on whole grains, lean protein, and leafy greens. A low-GI diet helps manage insulin resistance common in PCOS.
Regular exercise
30 minutes of moderate activity daily helps regulate hormones and improve insulin sensitivity. Walking, yoga, and swimming are particularly beneficial.
Quality sleep
7 to 9 hours of sleep per night helps regulate cortisol and reproductive hormones. Poor sleep is linked to worsening PCOS symptoms and insulin resistance.
Manage stress
Chronic stress raises cortisol which disrupts hormonal balance. Meditation, deep breathing and mindfulness have shown measurable improvements in PCOS symptoms.
Healthy weight
Even a 5–10% reduction in body weight can restore menstrual regularity and reduce androgen levels in women with PCOS who are overweight.
Regular monitoring
Track your menstrual cycles, hormonal levels and blood sugar. Regular check-ups with your doctor help catch complications like diabetes or thyroid issues early.
